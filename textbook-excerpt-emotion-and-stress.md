## Introduction to emotion and stress

*(Same introduction scene as above: campfire, calm context → sudden fear trigger.)*

Now that we've grasped why we experience emotions, let's delve into how we experience them. Conventional wisdom suggests that our emotional journey follows a linear sequence: an emotional event triggers our feelings, which then provokes an appropriate physiological response, leading to behavioral changes.

However, this conventional wisdom may sometimes be incorrect because experiencing emotions may not be as straightforward as it seems. Through detailed research into autonomic responses to emotions, psychologists have uncovered that physiological arousal occurs concurrently with or even precedes our conscious perception of emotions.

Several competing theories explain the process of experiencing emotions: **James-Lange**, **Cannon-Bard**, **Schachter-Singer**, and **Lazarus**. Proficiency in these theories is fundamental for excelling in the Psychology/Sociology section of the exam!
